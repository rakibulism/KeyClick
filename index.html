Features

Small app, serious sound

Everything runs locally in a lightweight menu bar app — no Dock icon, no setup, no measurable performance impact.

🎧

Three switch profiles

Clicky Blue for a sharp, high-pitched click. Tactile Brown for a softer bump. Thocky Linear for that deep, lubed-switch thock.

🔤

Every key sounds unique

All 26 letters have their own sound. Home-row keys thock deeper, the top row clicks sharper, F and J are muted by their homing bumps, and corner keys ring slightly hollow.

⌘

Combo & modifier sounds

Modifiers layer their own click under each keystroke, Caps Lock gets distinct on/off toggles, and big combos like ⌘⌥⌃⇧ fire a satisfying chord.

⚡️

Instant response

Sounds are pre-loaded into memory and play in under 15 milliseconds — they keep up with the fastest burst typing without lag or stutter.

🎚

Independent volume

Key sounds get their own volume slider, separate from system volume. One click mutes everything when you need silence.

🚀

Set and forget

Enable Launch at Login and KeyClick quietly starts with your Mac — always there, never in the way.

Install

Up and running in two minutes

KeyClick requires macOS 13 (Ventura) or newer.

Download and unzip

Grab KeyClick.zip and double-click it in your Downloads folder — you'll get KeyClick.app.

Move it to Applications

Drag KeyClick.app into your Applications folder.

Allow the app to open

KeyClick is free and isn't signed with a paid Apple certificate, so macOS blocks the first launch. Open Terminal (⌘ Space, type “Terminal”) and paste this line, then press Return:

xattr -d com.apple.quarantine /Applications/KeyClick.app

Open KeyClick and grant Accessibility

Launch KeyClick from Applications — a ⌨️ icon appears in your menu bar. The app opens System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility for you: switch KeyClick on, and sounds start instantly. This standard permission only lets the app hear that a key was pressed so it can play a sound — nothing is recorded.

Trouble? The troubleshooting guide covers the common fixes in 30 seconds.

🔒 Private by design

KeyClick uses a listen-only event tap to play sounds — it never records, stores, reconstructs, or transmits anything you type. It makes zero network connections, and the entire source code is open for anyone to audit.
